Why Yar exists

Built so that no one is left unheard.

Yar started the way every good medical device starts. Someone needed it, and nothing else worked.

Our founder's grandmother had a stroke and entered rehab in Minnesota. She couldn't make herself understood. Her family couldn't know she was okay without standing in her room. So they stood in her room, every single day, because there was no other way.

Yar is the other way.

Real people, with real problems, deserve a real solution.

"We've tried everything. It still doesn't feel like she's cared for unless I'm in the room." A daughter, before Yar
How it works

Three promises to every family.

Yar is always on. It sees and hears the room the way a good nurse would, understands what matters, and keeps none of it. No footage. No archive. Just the right message to the right person, the moment it counts.

1

Know, without asking.

The care team already records the day: meals, sleep, showers, medications, mood. Yar turns their notes into a clear, gentle feed on your phone, in your language. Nurses do nothing new. You call no one. And when nothing happened, Yar says so: "Nothing unusual today." Silence never means uncertainty.

Be there, from anywhere.

Approved family can drop in by voice or video. She answers nothing, presses nothing. Every drop-in announces itself out loud before connecting, staff can pause it with one tap during care, and Yar stores no footage. There is nothing to replay, and the device says so right on it.

For facilities

Be the facility families ask for by name.

When families stay notified, the phones ring less. When the phones ring less, your nurses move faster. When your nurses move faster, care gets better and the reviews follow. One device, three wins.

Faster workflow, calmer floor.

Every "how is she doing" call your nurses don't take is minutes back at the bedside. Families see the day as it happens, so they stop calling to reconstruct it.

Zero new documentation.

Yar works from what your staff already charts. It reads. It never adds a field, a form, or a step.

A communication record that protects your staff.

Every update a family receives is logged and exportable. When a complaint reaches patient relations, your team has the receipt.

Care preferences with a nurse in the loop.

Families suggest, your staff approves with one tap, every shift sees it. Preferences reach the bedside before the moment, not during it.

The family voice as a care tool.

Clinical trials show family presence reduces agitation and delirium. Yar lets family de-escalate at 3 a.m. and encourage therapy remotely, so your staff stop facing those moments alone.
